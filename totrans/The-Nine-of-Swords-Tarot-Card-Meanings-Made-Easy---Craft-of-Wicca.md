## The Nine of Swords tarot card description

The Nine of Swords tarot card often depicts a figure sitting up in bed, holding their head in their hands, and surrounded by nine swords hanging on the wall behind them.

The figure is usually depicted as being in a state of distress, with a look of fear or worry on their face. The nine swords symbolize fear, anxiety, guilt, and trauma, and suggest that the figure is consumed by these negative emotions.

 In some tarot decks, the figure is shown crying or with tears streaming down their face, further emphasizing the emotional turmoil they are experiencing. The swords may also be depicted as pointing towards the figure, reinforcing the idea that their fears and anxieties are closing in on them.

The bed and bedclothes may be depicted as disheveled, suggesting that the figure has been tossing and turning, unable to find peace in sleep. The surroundings may be dark and ominous, with clouds or a full moon in the background, adding to the overall mood of fear and anxiety.

 Overall, the Nine of Swords is considered a negative card, symbolizing internal turmoil and the inability to escape one’s own fears and anxieties. However, it can also indicate that the figure is in the process of confronting these emotions and working towards finding peace and resolution.

### Astrology of the Nine of Swords card

In tarot, each card is often associated with an astrological sign or planet. The exact correspondences can vary between tarot decks, but the Nine of Swords is often linked with the planet Mars and the zodiac sign of Pisces.

Mars is associated with courage, assertiveness, and action, but also with violence, aggression, and conflict. In the context of the Nine of Swords, Mars represents the internal conflicts and battles the figure is facing.

Pisces, on the other hand, is a water sign associated with emotions, intuition, and spirituality. In this card, Pisces represents the emotional distress and feelings of fear and anxiety experienced by the figure.

 The combination of Mars and Pisces in the Nine of Swords suggests a struggle between the desire for action and the need for emotional release and healing.

The card can indicate that the figure is grappling with their own inner demons and trying to find a way to overcome their fears and anxieties. The card may also suggest the need for a more spiritual or emotional approach to resolving internal conflicts, rather than relying on brute force or aggression.

## Upright Nine of Swords tarot card meaning

What is the meaning of the upright Nine of Swords card?

 **The upright Nine of Swords tarot card represents fear, anxiety, and guilt, and suggests the person is struggling with their own thoughts and fears. Despite the negative emotions depicted, the card may also indicate that the person is on the way to finding peace and resolution as they work to confront their fears and anxieties.**

The Nine of Swords tarot card is often seen as a symbol of fear, anxiety, and guilt. When this card appears in a tarot reading, it may indicate that the person is struggling with their own thoughts and fears, leading to feelings of unease and distress.

The figure in the card is usually depicted in a state of distress, with a look of fear or worry on their face. The nine swords hanging on the wall behind the figure symbolize the negative emotions and worries that are plaguing the person.

 These fears may be causing the person to have nightmares or vivid, frightening dreams, which are impacting their waking life.

However, the Nine of Swords card can also be a sign of hope and change. Despite the negative emotions depicted, the card may indicate that the person is in the process of confronting their fears and anxieties, and working towards finding peace and resolution.

The figure may be seen as taking a step towards healing, even if they still feel overwhelmed at the moment.

 Overall, the Nine of Swords represents the struggle with one’s own negative thoughts and emotions, but it can also suggest that help is on the horizon and that the person will find a way to overcome their fears.

However, it’s important to remember that tarot cards can offer symbolic guidance, rather than specific predictions or solutions.

The Nine of Swords may indicate that Lorraine is feeling overwhelmed by her thoughts and emotions, but it may also suggest that she is on the path to finding peace and resolution as she works to confront her fears and anxieties about her son’s career.

## Reversed Nine of Swords card tarot meaning

What is the meaning of the reversed Nine of Swords tarot card?

**The reversed Nine of Swords tarot card represents a shift towards hope and positivity, suggesting that the person has found relief from their negative thoughts and worries and is moving towards a more positive mindset. The card indicates a newfound sense of power and control over one’s thoughts and emotions, and a sense of peace and calm.**

 The reversed Nine of Swords tarot card can indicate a shift in the person’s outlook, away from fear and anxiety towards hope and optimism.

 When this card appears reversed in a tarot reading, it may suggest that the person has found a way to overcome their negative thoughts and worries, and is moving towards a more positive mindset.

In a more specific context, the reversed Nine of Swords card can indicate that the person has found relief from their nightmares or vivid, frightening dreams, and is experiencing a sense of peace and calm.

Overall, the reversed Nine of Swords card represents a shift towards hope and positivity, as the person moves past their fears and anxieties and begins to experience peace and calm.
