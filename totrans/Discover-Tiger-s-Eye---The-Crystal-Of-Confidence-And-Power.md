## About Tiger’s Eye

Tiger’s eye is thought to be an intergrowth of crocidolite and quartz. Overtime the crystal oxidises which causes it to become brown.

Tiger’s eye was a popular crystal in ancient Egypt and was often used to make scarab shaped amulets which were thought to offer the wearer protection. The idea that Tiger’s eye offered protective energies was also accepted by the Romans with Roman soldiers wearing the crystal as a protective talisman during battle. Tiger’s eye was also an important commodity in ancient China and the crystal was used to create prized decorative objects and jewellery

Tiger’s eye is generally multicoloured with its shades ranging from brown, reddish-brown or golden yellow. It typically has a striped or wavy pattern and a silky sheen. The gorgeous multifaceted beauty of Tiger’s eye makes it a particularity attractive crystal for displaying on your altar or bedside table don’t you think?
